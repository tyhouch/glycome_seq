CC   -!- FUNCTION: This protein might function as a defense against chitin
CC       containing pathogens. Binds to several branched or linear N-
CC       acetyllactosamine-containing glycosphingolipids and also to
CC       lactosylceramide with sphingosine and non-hydroxy fatty acids.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homodimer. {ECO:0000305}.
CC   -!- PTM: Heavily glycosylated with beta-arabinose on hydroxyprolines and
CC       with alpha-galactose on serines of the extensin-like domain. As no
CC       other sugars could be detected in the native lectin, it is unlikely
CC       that the three putative N-glycosylation sites are actually
CC       glycosylated. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:666730}.
CC   -!- PTM: The N-terminus is blocked. The N-terminal sequences proposed in
CC       PubMed:9022287 and PubMed:11056399 originate probably from truncated
CC       proteins.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: In the central section; belongs to the extensin family.
CC       {ECO:0000305}.
